About Me

I am a dedicated Lecturer with a passion for advancing the field of Information Technology through both teaching and research. With over four years of experience, I have had the privilege of educating students in key areas such as Software Engineering, Operating Systems, Mobile Application Development, and Database Systems. My academic journey is defined by a commitment to fostering student success, whether through dynamic classroom delivery or guiding them on their final-year projects.

Beyond teaching, my research is focused on the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and education, with a particular interest in exploring AI-driven solutions for assessment, learning analytics, and curriculum design. I am also involved in examining the socio-technical implications of Generative AI, specifically its impact on teachers' workload, curriculum practices, and professional judgment.

Throughout my career, I have been involved in various innovative projects, including AI-based applications, mobile app development, and smart systems. These experiences have shaped my approach to education and research, with a strong emphasis on practical, real-world applications.

In addition to my academic and research pursuits, I strive to maintain an active presence in the broader tech community through conference presentations and publications, always seeking to contribute to the conversation around AI's role in the future of education.

Research Projects

SpeechBuddy: AI-Powered Gamified Speech Therapy Application

Ongoing Research Project (MSc)

Investigating the use of Generative AI-supported systems for speech therapy and inclusive educational contexts, with attention to engagement, feedback, and ethical considerations. Focuses on improving engagement, pronunciation accuracy, and adaptive feedback through intelligent speech analysis. The research explores AI-assisted therapy tools in supporting inclusive and accessible educational and clinical interventions.

Smart Mirror for Autistic Education - Aliza

Undergraduate Final Year Research Project

Designed and implemented an intelligent smart mirror using facial expression and emotion recognition to support autistic children in daily routines and learning activities. Evaluated an AI-enabled smart mirror system to support autistic children's learning routines and engagement. This project resulted in a peer-reviewed IEEE conference publication and was a finalist at NBQSA 2020.
